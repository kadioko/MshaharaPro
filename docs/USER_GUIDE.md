# MshaharaPro User Guide

## What MshaharaPro Helps With

MshaharaPro helps Tanzanian businesses and accountants manage monthly payroll, statutory deductions, payslips, compliance reports, approvals, and audit-ready records.

It is built for teams that need payroll to be simple, organized, and reviewable.

## Who It Helps

### SMEs and Business Owners

MshaharaPro helps owners understand payroll costs, employee payments, statutory obligations, and approval status without managing everything in spreadsheets.

Useful for:

- Tracking monthly payroll status
- Reviewing gross pay, net pay, and employer cost
- Approving payroll before payment
- Keeping payroll records organized
- Preparing for accountant or tax advisor review

### Accountants and Payroll Consultants

Accountants can manage multiple client companies from one dashboard.

Useful for:

- Seeing all client payroll statuses in one place
- Finding missing employee data quickly
- Preparing PAYE, NSSF, WCF, and SDL reports
- Generating payslips for client employees
- Keeping audit logs for payroll changes
- Standardizing payroll work across many clients

### Payroll Managers

Payroll managers can maintain employees, calculate payroll, add approved adjustments, generate payslips, and prepare reports.

Useful for:

- Employee data management
- Salary and allowance tracking
- Manual adjustments with reasons
- Payroll calculation and review
- Payslip generation
- Payment schedule preparation

### Employees

Employees can access their own payroll profile and payslip information without seeing other employees' data.

Useful for:

- Viewing personal payroll details
- Checking payslip availability
- Reviewing missing profile information
- Accessing employee documents where enabled

### Platform Admins

Platform admins manage system-level controls such as payroll rules, tenant oversight, and platform configuration.

Useful for:

- Managing statutory rule configuration
- Reviewing rule version changes
- Supporting organizations and accountants
- Protecting admin-only settings

## Main Workflows

### 1. Company Setup

Add company information such as:

- Company name
- TIN and optional VRN
- NSSF employer number
- WCF registration number
- TRA tax region
- Business sector
- Employee count
- Payroll month preferences
- SDL applicability
- Company logo

### 2. Employee Management

Add and maintain:

- Employee number and full name
- Contact information
- NIDA, TIN, and NSSF number
- Job title and department
- Employment type
- Basic salary and allowances
- Bank or mobile-money details
- Active/inactive status

### 3. Payroll Rules

Payroll rules are configurable so rates, thresholds, formulas, and effective dates can be updated later.

Initial configurable rules include:

- PAYE placeholder brackets
- NSSF contribution settings
- WCF rate
- SDL rate
- SDL employee-count threshold

Payroll rules should always be reviewed by a qualified accountant or tax advisor before use for official submissions.

### 4. Payroll Run

Typical monthly flow:

1. Select company and month
2. Load active employees
3. Calculate gross pay, deductions, contributions, and net pay
4. Review missing employee data warnings
5. Add manual adjustments with reasons
6. Save payroll
7. Submit for approval
8. Approve and lock payroll
9. Generate payslips and reports
10. Mark payroll as paid

### 5. Reports

MshaharaPro supports payroll exports such as:

- Payroll summary
- Payroll register
- PAYE report
- NSSF report
- WCF report
- SDL report
- Bank payment schedule
- Department cost report
- Loan/advance report

Final statutory report formats should be confirmed with a qualified local expert before production filing.

### 6. Audit Logs

Important actions are logged, including:

- Employee creation or updates
- Salary changes
- Payroll calculations
- Manual adjustments
- Payroll submission and approval
- Payroll locking
- Payslip generation
- Report exports

This helps teams keep payroll records ready for review.

## Recommended User Roles

- Platform Admin: system owner or internal product operator
- Accountant: external consultant managing multiple companies
- Company Owner: business owner or director approving payroll
- Payroll Manager: HR/payroll staff preparing monthly payroll
- Employee: self-service profile and payslip access

## Best Practices

- Keep TIN, NSSF, NIDA, and bank details complete before payroll calculation
- Review payroll warnings before submitting for approval
- Do not change payroll rules without documenting the source and effective date
- Lock payroll after approval and payment
- Export and store reports monthly
- Have a qualified accountant or tax advisor review calculations before submission

## Important Disclaimer

Payroll calculations should be reviewed by a qualified accountant or tax advisor before submission.
